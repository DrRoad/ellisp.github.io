    I write about using real data for real purposes. I show how to access and use data, and provide examples of semi-finished products and the code that produced them. 'In the wild' is metaphorical - I don't write in particular about natural wilderness (although am happy to if the data comes up), as much as the rugged but human-made world of compromise, pragmatism, and difficult practical choices that is real-world data analysis. Occasionally I give extra explanation of published things I have had input to in my day job, but everything written here is my own opinion and on my own responsibility.

    Importing the New Zealand Income Survey SURF

    15 August 2015

    I tidy up the publicly available simulated unit record file (SURF) of the New Zealand Income Survey 2011, import into a database, and explore income distributions, visualising the lower distribution of weekly incomes New Zealanders of Maori and Pacific Islander ethnicity.
